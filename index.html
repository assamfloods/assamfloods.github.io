    Natural Calamities come in all forms and hard times have a way to unite people. They rise above their differences for a cause much bigger than their individual needs. But times like these are also not free from the diabolical footprint. This "Gabbar is Back" report is an attempt to highlight the "other side" of flood relief fundraisers, portraying an account of objective observations centered around the activity of one such group (People for People), which is indicative of the beginning of a problem that has the potency to spread out in the coming years.

    The key findings of the report include the following points:

    • The group People for People (also goes by as P4P, PFP, Manuhe manuhor babe) is an unregistered entity initially founded to provide relief to the victims of Bhagjan OIL incidence. After raising a sum of Rs 1.19 lakhs items such as clothes, books, food materials were distributed, in addition to the items donated by various individuals, on 18th June 2020. This group has been very vocal about their transparency and hence receipts included in the google drive can be found, available publicly on their social media pages. However, in this drive, a sum of Rs. 30,630 was allegedly distributed among 16 worst affected families, though there is no evidence that proves that claim.
    • Gaining from local traction and harnessing the power of social media, this group began building trust among the donors and started to reach out to more and more organizations/individuals/institutes. Some key donors were employees of Bank of Baroda (donated 1.25 lakhs), BTS Army (Rs. 5.5 lakhs), Connecting the Dots Foundation (donated various materials), OIL (provided cargo truck), XIME Chennai, etc. Various ways to raise funds were organized such as the Milaap campaign, personal donation, and organizing E-concerts. A total sum of Rs. 16,39,632 was allegedly collected after standard deductions, till 26th July 2020. (there is no account of the funds collected afterward except for Rs. 16,600)
    • Rs. 50,510 and Rs. 5,58,504 was used in the Bogoribari village drive (31st July 2020) and Jonai Drive (10th August 2020) respectively, apart from the items obtained from Connecting the Dots Foundation. Thus, a total sum of Rs. 10,30,618 is supposedly the remaining amount (plus Rs. 16,600). (see the "Chronology and overall funds auditing" section for complete details)
    • In all these drives, despite their claims of understanding the situation, there was a clear violation of the Covid-19 guidelines. In many of the incidences, the team can be seen mingling with the local population, not wearing masks, breaching social distancing norms, and indulging in hospitality. The group had been taking the entire team (28-30 people) to these drives which not only overshoot the expenses, which could have been utilized otherwise but has also risked the health of the local community. A document named "Evidence, Sadiya" given in the sub-section "About safety measures" under the section of "Observations" clearly defines the carelessness during the Covid-19 emergency.

    • Drives conducted by this group naturally required procurement of the items from different vendors, again the receipts and invoices were public knowledge. However, scrutinizing these vendors revealed fraudulent activities. A certain trader named "Nazrana Trader" had a fake GST/TIN number. And another major vendor "MAA Traders" was found to be involved in faking the bills in terms of dates and the price of the items, and were also identified to be benefiting the buyer (i.e. People for People) from the commission on each item. "MAA Traders" initially denied to be a retailer of items such as "mosquito net" when inquired for the same on the phone but was found to be in possession of 3 different letterheads for electronic items and mosquito nets, when the in-person visit was made. They have also mentioned how some individuals of this group (P4P) were benefiting from this arrangement. (for the detailed report and audio clips refer to sub-section "About MAA Traders" under the section "Observation")
    • Other red flags include the evidence of not using leftover items in the subsequent drive, providing relief to 300 and 700 families (occurrence of families in whole numbers are statistically impossible), and having no account of funds raised after 26th July.

    These key identifiers suggest that the forefront of a noble deed can have roots in individual greed, and also how such calamities proves as an opportunity for such unchecked entities. It thus becomes imperative to scrutinize such unregistered groups that are actively getting away with breaching donor's trust, fraud, and endangerment to lives.

Background

Assam is the 16th largest state in India, while it can be even compared to the size of an entire country itself 'Czech Republic'. The state receives an average annual rainfall of "1,524.6 mm" between the months of April till October, every year. This combined with the swelling of Brahmaputra and other tributaries wreak havoc on the local population, particularly those in low lying areas and villages established in flood plains. Even though it is a yearly phenomenon, the national media hardly paid attention to this - compared to the attention other regions receive. There is definitely a preparedness seen in the local population - which is at the forefront of this massive & unstoppable natural disaster. However, with growing temperatures and climate change - the floods are getting bigger and have started affecting areas that aren't usually affected, for e.g. District Chirang, Sibsagar, etc.

Owing to this, the development sector / local NGOs have been raising funds from local and foreign sources for providing relief in different areas to households affected. This work is ongoing for years now, most of it being done by NGOs established locally by few good Samaritans - also supported by people and organizations from across the northeast and other parts of India. Due to improved Internet connectivity and mobile penetration across many regions, a lot of 'live' and 'real' conditions come out directly with the help of Social media platforms, leading to many Individuals and 'groups' successfully able to raise funds for flood relief related causes. Despite all of this, the fact that a large portion of such funds is misused in various ways including direct 'pocketing' of money in many instances. Even though the Disaster Management Act and other laws provide for several punishments in case of 'discrepancies' - very often no action is taken by the local administration.

Introduction

This report titled 'Gabbar is Back' highlights the journey of observations into an 'unregistered', 'virtually created and run' GROUP called People for People, based on records available in public domain including Social media platforms, official media reports, telephone calls recordings, audio recordings conducted during physical visits. The details were procured or at one point of time when they were available - however we cannot guarantee if they are not yet removed from the respective social media platforms.

People for People (often called P4P or PFP) has no registration with any government agency including Income Tax Department. The group does not display any certificate of registration / PAN number / GST / TIN number, on any of their social media pages, nor do they claim to have a valid registration anywhere. As such, lack of registration with government is not enough bona fide reasoning for such an investigation, yet all along the way we found compelling evidence to proceed towards a detailed inquiry of each and every step to uncover the truth behind details presented in public.

Methodology

Intention behind Investigation

The why is often very interesting, without which everything in life seems meaningless. Why are we really trying to uncover something which looks like a private relationship between donors and the group. While it is definitely a donor's choice to 'contribute' their resources & money to anyone freely, 'donated' with blind trust on how such campaigns are eventually 'designed' and 'marketed' online. In cases of unorganized groups, it is virtually impossible to establish an Audited accounts or verification by certified Accountants or any other government agency, except for when an FIR is filed with the Police.

The intention has been always CLEAR. Its multi-fold indeed. It is protection of the donor's trust on their counterparts both morally as well as ethically. It is also ensuring that affected populations are not deprived of what is due to them - without corruption in the middle. It is also to ensure that 'grassroot social workers' who invest their years sacrificing a lot, just for the larger benefit of the society are not made to look 'stupid' due to such kind of 'campaigns'.

Closing

Although the initial focus of the inquiry was focussed towards recording irregularities occurred due to lack of experience as a group as well as no presence of experienced social worker or grassroots worker, the latter part of this inquiry started showing hints of multiple kinds of issues including financial irregularities by those procuring material.

We request the administration / competent authority / journalists / donors to further look into loose ends as we could only demonstrate as much as public information was available. Once this case is pursued further by the 'rightful' entities, it is surely going to reveal a lot more.

Last, but not the least, it is highly unfortunate to see such practices happening in the name of "relief work", however as our experiences tell us, it is not new. So far, such practices continue without any over-sight and hence we are unable to bring to light those who indulges themselves without an iota of fear. But now, no more. Such acts must be uncovered, and they shall be, in future too.
